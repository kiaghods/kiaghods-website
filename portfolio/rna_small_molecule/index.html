RNA Small Molecule Discovery @ UF Scripps, Disney Lab

During my senior year of high school, I was fortunate enough to be a research intern at The Herbert Wertheim UF Scripps Institute for Biomedical Innovation & Technology, working in the lab of Dr. Matthew Disney.

Worked on a wide array of both computational and wet-lab projects, including:

  • Contributed to the expansion of Inforna - a database matching small molecules to RNA secondary structural motifs – to include 194 new molecules and 82 new motifs
  • Synthesized covalent binders to target cancer-linked c-Myc RNA and repeated genetic patterns (i.e., r(CAG) expansion) (View Abstract)
  • Screened dozens of small molecule binders against the frame-shifting element of SARS-CoV-2 RNA in HEK293-T cells
